1. Volcanoes
A volcano is an opening in the Earth’s surface where lava, gas, and ash escape. There are 3 types: active, dormant, and extinct. Some erupt suddenly, while others release lava slowly. Temperatures can reach over 1,000°C. Volcanoes can destroy areas but also create new land. The soil around them is often rich and good for farming.

2. Earthquakes
An earthquake is the shaking of the ground caused by movement in the Earth’s crust. It happens along faults where rocks break. Earthquakes are measured using the Richter scale, from 1 to 10+. Strong ones above 7 can cause major damage. They may last a few seconds but affect large areas.

3. Hurricanes
A hurricane is a large storm that forms over warm ocean water. Wind speeds can exceed 119 km/h. Hurricanes are classified into 5 categories. They bring heavy rain, strong winds, and flooding. These storms can last several days and weaken when they reach land. Warning systems help people prepare in advance.

4. Tornadoes
A tornado is a spinning column of air that touches the ground. Winds can reach over 300 km/h. Tornadoes usually form during thunderstorms. They can be 100 meters wide or more. Most last less than 10 minutes. Tornadoes can destroy buildings and trees quickly. Radar systems help detect them early.

5. Rainbows
A rainbow forms when sunlight passes through water droplets. The light splits into 7 colors: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet. It appears as an arc in the sky. Rainbows usually form after rain when the sun shines. The angle of about 42 degrees is needed to see it clearly.

6. Lightning
Lightning is a powerful electrical discharge during storms. It can reach temperatures of about 30,000°C. Lightning travels very fast and is followed by thunder. It occurs when charges build up in clouds. Each year, millions of lightning strikes happen worldwide. It can be dangerous and cause fires or injuries.

7. Floods
A flood happens when water covers land that is normally dry. It can result from heavy rain, storms, or river overflow. Some floods occur in a few hours, while others take days. Water levels can rise several meters. Floods can damage homes, roads, and crops. Protective systems help reduce risk.

8. Droughts
A drought is a long period with little or no rainfall. It can last months or even years. During droughts, water levels drop and crops fail. Temperatures are often high, above 30°C. This affects food production and ecosystems. Water conservation is important during drought conditions.

9. Tsunamis
A tsunami is a series of ocean waves caused by underwater earthquakes or eruptions. Waves can travel at 800 km/h in deep water. Near the coast, they grow taller, sometimes over 10 meters. Tsunamis can flood large areas and cause destruction. Warning systems help alert coastal regions.

10. Aurora Borealis
The aurora borealis is a natural light display in the sky. It occurs near the poles. Colors include green, red, and purple. It is caused by particles from the sun. These lights appear about 80 to 300 km above Earth. They are best seen at night during clear weather.
